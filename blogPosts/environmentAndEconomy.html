Will targets to bring greenhouse gas emissions in the UK to net zero by 2050 be a drag on economic growth?

Question taken from here: https://www.res.org.uk/resources-page/young-economist-of-the-year-winners-announced.html
Photo by Singkham from Pexels


Bringing down greenhouse emissions to net zero is not an easy task, requiring extensive regulation to encourage businesses to shift away from environmentally unfriendly products and raw materials.


But what exactly does “net zero” mean? It means that the volume of greenhouse gas emissions is equal to the volume removed from the atmosphere. This therefore means that your net emissions are zero, because you produce and remove the same amount.
The primary challenge is not even bringing greenhouse gas emissions to net zero, because once there is a roadmap to net zero emissions it is straightforward, rather the challenge is to make this roadmap. There are multiple ways to reduce greenhouse gas emissions to net zero, I mean there’s the obvious approach of suddenly just not having any power stations fuelled by fossil fuels, but that has far reaching consequences due to the fact that over 50% of our energy is generated through fossil fuels[1]. Another approach is to increase our reliance on nuclear energy instead, but the nuclear waste is extremely dangerous, among other issues such as the potential of a nuclear meltdown. However, for the purpose of this essay it is assumed that “Environmental regulations” means the abandonment of fossil fuel powered energy in an instant.
Furthermore, what exactly is a “drag” on economic growth? Is it as simple as just slowing down economic growth? Does it only mean short term or long term too? If it does mean long term, then how can we reliably predict what the economic growth would’ve been like without environmental regulations a hundred, two hundred years after the UK becomes net zero? And regardless, even if being net zero in terms of greenhouse emissions is a drag on economic growth, the trade-off is worth it.
In addition, so far, we’ve been discussing as if the targets have actually been achieved. The targets themselves will do nothing; the effect comes from actually achieving those targets. However, it will be assumed in this essay that the targets are achieved.
