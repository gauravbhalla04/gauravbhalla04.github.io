About

My Journey to academic success at AGSB

  • 2018

    My first GCSE

    It was at the end of Year 8 that I sat my first GCSE; I think this was crucial in setting me up for future academic success, as I didn't feel any pressure in the exam. This was largely due to my familiarity with the subject, Punjabi, however it allowed me to become familiar with exams without feeling any pressure. This is, I believe, the reason why I am able to cope with high pressure situations now. I achieved an A* in the GCSE, with my overall percent being 96%.

  • 2020

    Further Academic success

    At the end of Year 10, I achieved two more GCSEs, both grade 9s. I sat GCSE maths and GCSE PE, and each have their own demands. When it was announced that official exams would not go ahead, I was one of the students who didn't worry at all, because I had not relied on performing only in the final exams, but rather pushed myself to be at my best throughout the entire year. I believe in aiming for excellence at every opportunity, rather than success at the last possible moment. If you aim for excellence, success will follow.

  • 2021

    A Full Set

    In August 2021 I will recieve my GCSE grades for all my subjects, along with grades for the OCR FSMQ and an AS level in Religous Studies. I am currently predicted all 9s for my GCSEs, and an A in both of my other qualifications, and both are graded A-E
